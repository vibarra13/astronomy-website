About me!

My name is Victor Ibarra-Martinez. I started learning astronomy at a very young age when I got my first telescope, and I have recently discovered that passion again. I made this website to showcase my skills in front end developing using CSS and HTML, and to showcase the images I have taken with camera and telescope. I'm always looking to learn new things because I love to learn!

At the moment, I'm working on getting my bachelor's degree in computer science at a 4 year university. As of December 2021 I am not in school, but being a recent community college graduate with my associates degree I want to transfer to a 4 year school to continue my studies. I hope to build experience in astronomy and computer science while I'm at school, and my overall dream is to work with NASA. I can code in Python, Java, CSS, and HTML.

I am currently working on a project that allows me to collect image data from radio telescopes. I will write code that can read the data from FITS files, pixel by pixel, and it will determine whether there is a pulsar within that image, and it will locate it on the image. I take great pride in what I do, and I hope to work with others on even bigger projects.

You can reach me on my socials underneath the picture of me! Thanks for reading!
